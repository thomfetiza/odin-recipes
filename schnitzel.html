Wiener Schnitzel Recipe

In its most basic form, schnitzel is a slice of meat, often pounded until fairly thin, then breaded and fried. The dish's name actually comes from the German word "schnitt," which translates to "cut." The most popular type of meat for schnitzel is veal (wiener-schnitzel) or pork (schweine-schnitzel), but you also see varieties made with chicken (h√§nchen-schnitzel), or turkey (puten-schnitzel). The breading is typically an easy coating of flour, egg wash, and breadcrumbs. Schnitzel is typically not highly seasoned, relying instead on bold toppings like fresh lemon wedges, mushroom sauce, or cream sauce to add a boost of flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds veal cutlets
  • 1.5 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon minced parsley
  • 1 teaspoon minced parsley
  • 0.5 teaspoon salt
  • 0.25 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1 cup dry breadcrumbs
  • 6 tablespoons butter
  • 4 slices lemon

Steps

  1. Cut the veal into steaks, about as thick as your finger. Dredge in flour. In a shallow dish, beat the eggs with 1 tablespoon oil, salt and pepper. Coat the veal with egg mixture, then with bread crumbs.
  2. Heat 1/4 cup oil in a heavy skillet over medium heat. Fry veal until golden brown, about 5 minutes on each side.
